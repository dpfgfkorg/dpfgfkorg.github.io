Chitty

"A Trial of Blood, Sweat and Tears"

It's hard to believe it has been over 5 years since we embarked on the Chitty Project... At the time myself and Dean were Ventures and Aron had but a year left in the Scout Section. The idea blossomed from the journey home from our week long summer camp in Portlick in July 2010. After our success in the 2010 Star Scout Show that March, we decided we need to go bigger and better! The idea of replicating the chariot scenes in BenHur was joked about as was the notion of building a model Chitty. It wasn't until the usual "end of summer" camp between myself, Dean and Aron did we really consider the ambigious task of bullding our own Chitty! By the end of the following week, plans for the car had been drawn in masking tape on the floor of the den and a list of parts required to build her had been drawn up.

A project of this scale had never before been attempted in 5th Carlow, nor has anything comparable been done since. A meeting was called between ourselves and the parents of 5th Carlow, we'd one chance to show them our enthusiasm, our leading abilities in this endeavour and the oppertunities that would come from this for their child. The meeting ensuded, myself and Dean had rehersed the presentation over and over and dealt with questions as they came.Needless so say, we were successful in our endeavour, otherwise I wouldnt be writing this! As we came to the end of our practiced speeches, it was time to put the cherry on the cake as Aron flicked the switch of the 1/6th Scale model Chitty we had built and hidden behind the projector screen. Its lights flared into life as the screen rose up, we'd won the crowd over long before their applause.

We had zero budget, not even enough for the tea and biscuits during the weekly rehersals! We relied on what little we could find in our garages and sheds, and those of our friends, family and neighbours. We sought donations from construction suppliers to the parents themselves and returned with some, but nowhere near as much as we hoped. Despite this, we plowed on, putting in our own money to cover the essential materials we needed. It was tough, the Winter of 2010 was the Winter of the bad snow. It was cold perpetually.
